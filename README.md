## Overview

**Nurolab** is an interdisciplinary initiative focused on developing intelligent systems for understanding cognitive and mental well-being through **Electroencephalography (EEG)**, **Artificial Intelligence (AI)**, and **Human-Centered Technology**.

The project brings together:

* Neuroscience
* Artificial Intelligence & Machine Learning
* Signal Processing
* Hardware Product Design

Our goal is to complement traditional subjective assessments with objective physiological measurements that may provide additional insights into cognitive and mental states.

---

## Problem Statement

Mental health challenges affect millions of people worldwide.

Current assessment approaches often rely heavily on:

* Self-reported experiences
* Clinical interviews
* Behavioral observations

While these methods are essential, there remains a need for tools that can support continuous, data-driven monitoring of cognitive and physiological indicators.

This may help improve:

* Self-awareness
* Longitudinal monitoring
* Research into mental well-being
* Early identification of patterns that may warrant further evaluation

---

## Our Approach

We aim to develop systems that:

1. Measure brain activity using EEG
2. Process and analyze neural signals
3. Investigate EEG patterns associated with cognitive and emotional states
4. Generate interpretable insights using AI-driven analytics
5. Support researchers, clinicians, and users through data-informed decision making

---

## Why EEG?

EEG is a non-invasive technique that measures electrical activity generated by the brain.

Potential advantages include:

* Real-time neural activity monitoring
* Relatively low-cost data acquisition
* High temporal resolution
* Portability and wearable-device compatibility

The project explores features such as:

* Alpha, Beta, Theta, and Delta activity
* Spectral power characteristics
* Functional connectivity and coherence
* Event-related neural patterns
* Cognitive workload indicators

## Scientific Limitations

It is important to recognize that:

* EEG alone cannot diagnose psychiatric disorders.
* Mental health assessment requires qualified clinical evaluation.
* EEG biomarkers for many psychological conditions remain an active area of research.
* AI predictions should be interpreted as supportive information rather than medical conclusions.

---

## Challenges
